Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

1931 - 2015

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, popularly known as A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, was an Indian scientist and the 11th President of India. He was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India, and passed away on July 27, 2015.

Dr. Kalam played a pivotal role in India's missile and nuclear programs and was often referred to as the "Missile Man of India." He was an inspirational figure who believed in the power of education and youth to transform the nation.

His vision, dedication, and contributions to science and technology continue to inspire people around the world. Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam's life and work are a shining example of how a person can make a significant impact on society.
